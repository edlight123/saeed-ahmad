Profile

Building legal frameworks that protect expression and faith

A strategist bridging scholarship, litigation, and mentorship pathways.

Saeed Ahmad is a Harvard Law School J.D. '24 and Visiting Fellow with the Harvard Law School Religious Freedom Clinic. He centers pluralism and creative expression while advising institutions on constitutional governance and intellectual property.

His experience spans clerkship service on the Supreme Court of Pakistan, corporate practice in New York and Los Angeles, and research that advances the fashion industry's legal protections. Saeed partners with clients to align mission, culture, and compliance through thoughtful storytelling and principled advocacy.
