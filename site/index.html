Profile Overview

A legal strategist grounded in service, scholarship, and community leadership.

Saeed Ahmad is a Harvard Law School J.D. ’24 and Visiting Fellow with the Harvard Law School Religious Freedom Clinic. His practice centers on safeguarding constitutional liberties, championing pluralism, and advising institutions on the intersections of religious freedom, governance, and creative enterprise.

Drawing on clerkship experience at the Supreme Court of Pakistan and engagements with leading firms in New York and Los Angeles, Saeed blends comparative constitutional insight with commercial sophistication. His scholarship explores intellectual property protections in the fashion industry and the legal architectures that preserve cultural expression.
