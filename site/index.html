Meet Saeed

Saeed Ahmad is a Pakistani-American recent graduate of Harvard Law School, and was a recipient of the Dean’s Award for Community Leadership. He works as an attorney in New York City, while also serving as a Visiting Fellow at the Harvard Law School Religious Freedom Clinic. Saeed hails from the city of Corona in Southern California, earned 3 AA degrees from Norco College, & graduated with a B.A. degree in Psychology from UCLA in 2020. Before law school, he worked as a Client Advisor at the Louis Vuitton Flagship Store on Rodeo Drive.

His tenure in law school encompassed working as a summer clerk at the Supreme Court of Pakistan, a boutique commercial litigation firm in Los Angeles, and at a corporate law firm in New York City. Additionally, his involvements on campus spanned the Harvard Association for Law & Business, J.D. Admissions Fellow Program, Committee For Sports & Entertainment Law, & research focused on IP/Copyright law in the realm of fashion. He has also served as an editor on the Harvard Business Law Review.
