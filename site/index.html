About Me

Legal Scholar & Advocate

I am a recent graduate from Harvard Law School (J.D. '24) and currently serve as a Visiting Fellow at the Harvard Law School Religious Freedom Clinic. My academic and professional journey reflects a deep commitment to constitutional law, religious freedom, and intellectual property rights.

During my time at Harvard, I had the privilege of serving as an Editor for the Harvard Business Law Review and conducting research on IP/Copyright law in the fashion industry. I also clerked for Justice Shah at the Supreme Court of Pakistan and worked at prestigious law firms in New York and Los Angeles.

Prior to law school, I graduated from UCLA with a B.A. in Psychology, where I developed strong analytical and research skills that continue to inform my legal work today.
